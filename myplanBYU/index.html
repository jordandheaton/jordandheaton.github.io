How myplanBYU works

myplanBYU treats your degree as a constrained set-cover and sequencing problem. Unlike a static checklist, it generates an optimized multi-semester sequence around your actual life.

1 · Requirement buckets, not hardcoded lists

Every program (major, minor, certificate, University Core) is a set of buckets — “3 credits tagged GE-Arts”, “all of the IS Junior Core”, “6 credits of technique courses”. Courses carry tags and can fill multiple buckets at once (double-counting), up to a global credit cap. Programs without hand-entered detail generate realistic placeholder chains, so every one of the 170+ catalog programs is plannable today and refinable tomorrow.

2 · Hard constraints (never violated)

  • Prerequisite chains & repeatable-course sequencing
  • Season availability (Fall-only, Winter-only, rare offerings)
  • Per-term credit caps, full-time minimums for scholarships
  • Cohort blocks — e.g. the IS Junior Core moves as one Fall block, then one Winter block
  • Program gates — e.g. MISM application prerequisites before the deadline semester
  • Your pins — “IS 303 in Winter 2027” stays exactly there

3 · Weighted priorities (your dials)

The solver seeds a schedule greedily along the critical path, then runs ~1,600 rounds of hill-climbing: random legal moves are kept only if your weighted objective improves. Speed minimizes semesters; Cost packs the flat-tuition band, counts Spring/Summer tuition, and knows a 12-month lease makes Spring housing free; GPA protection refuses to stack 3+ historically hard classes; Workload balances real time-cost (a 1-credit technique class ≠ 1 credit of time); Life penalizes crammed terms, heavy streaks, and uneven religion pacing.

4 · Honest flags

Plans aren't just feasible — they're annotated: bottleneck courses vs. your registration priority, single-point-of-failure offerings, AP/CLEP test-out suggestions, finals-week collision risk, and scholarship full-time warnings.

Under the hood

100% client-side JavaScript (solves in ~10–50 ms for a full double-degree). The repo includes an OR-Tools CP-SAT reference architecture (docs/solver_reference.py) for a future server-side V2 with section-level time conflicts.
